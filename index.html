Maria Elena Walsh

These are some of the most important facts about the life of María Elena Walsh:

  • 1930
    María Elena Walsh was born in Villa Sarmiento, Morón, Buenos Aires, to a railway worker of English and Irish descent, and a woman of Spanish descent.
  • 1948
    She had some of her poems and her first book published. After being invited to North America and Europe during the era of Peronism, she moved to Paris.
  • 1955
    She met the woman who would become the love of her life, Sara Facio. However, their relationship began two years later when they reunited in Argentina.
  • 1958
    She wrote numerous TV scripts, plays, poems, books and songs. She was also a successful performer, singing her own songs onstage and recording them later in albums. She also made a satirical show for adults, which was made into a film of the same title.
  • 1985-1994
    In 1985 The film The Official Story won the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film. In 1985 she received the title of Illustrious Citizen of the City of Buenos Aires. In 1990 was named Doctor honoris causa of the National University of Cordoba and Illustrious People of Buenos Aires Province. In 1994 she was Highly Commended for the Hans Christian Andersen Award
